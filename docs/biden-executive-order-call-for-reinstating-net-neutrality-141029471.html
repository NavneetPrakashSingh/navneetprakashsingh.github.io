    Build, break, repeat

    Biden's wide-ranging executive order covers Big Tech, net neutrality and more | Engadget

    Similar notions of price transparency, consumer rights, increased scrutiny of mergers and prevention of excessive fees were prevalent across the other industries covered. The order contains 72 proposals and actions, among which it specifically says "the President encourages the FCC to restore Net Neutrality rules undone by the prior administration." In other industries, like banking and personal finance, the order similarly asked for more robust scrutiny of mergers. It also asked the agency to consider limiting early termination fees and prevent internet service providers from making deals with landlords that limit tenant choices. It also urged the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) to "issue rules allowing customers to download their banking data and take it with them." Under agriculture, for example, the order also highlighted the need to give consumers the right to repair their tractors and equipment. In other words, it's targeting anticompetitive practices across a wide range of industries, including internet services and tech.
